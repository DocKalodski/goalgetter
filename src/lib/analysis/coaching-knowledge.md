## What Coaching Is vs Is Not

| Coaching Is NOT | Coaching IS |
|-----------------|-------------|
| Giving Answers | Asking Questions |
| Lectures or Advice | Enabling Learning |
| Blaming | Finding Opportunities |
| Control | Empowerment & Responsibility |

> "Coaching is unlocking people's potential to maximize their own performance. It is helping them to learn rather than teaching them." — Sir John Whitmore

**Awareness (Internal):** Beliefs, Physical Experiences, Health, Emotions, Actions
**Awareness (External):** Results, Environment, People & Relationships
**Responsibility:** Choice & Consequences, Ownership & Accountability, Victim vs Responsible, Reasons vs Results

## Ego States (Eric Berne — Transactional Analysis)

| State | Stands For | Expression | Body Language |
|-------|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Parent | TAUGHT concept of life | "Always", "Never", "Should", "Must" | Finger pointing, patronizing |
| Adult | THOUGHT concept of life (data/facts) | "Why", "How", "I think", reasoned statements | Composed, calm, open |
| Child | FELT concept of life (internal reactions) | "I wish", "I don't care", "I want" | Shoulders shrugging, whining |

**PAC Functional Model:**
- Controlling Parent (CP): promotes rules, shoulds/musts
- Nurturing Parent (NP): acceptance, nurturing, caring
- Adult: responds to here-and-now logically
- Adapted Child (AC): complies or rebels to parental expectations
- Free Child (FC): spontaneity, creativity, independent of expectations

## Who Are You? (Carl Rogers — Humanistic Psychology)

- **Self-Image:** Actual behavior / how you see yourself
- **Ideal/Idealized Self:** Who you would like to be (may not match reality; "social media self")
- **Actualized Self:** Life in congruence with Ideal-Self and Self-Image; fulfilling potential

> "People are basically good. People have an actualizing tendency, the built-in motivation present in every life-form to develop its potentials to the fullest extent possible."

## The Art of Asking Questions

**Disempowering Questions:** Focus on reasons why a person did not or cannot succeed.
**Empowering Questions:** Get people to think and allow them to discover their own answers, developing self-responsibility.

**Tips:**
1. Observation/Data not Judgment — "At what part of the timeline did you encounter challenges?" (not "How come there are always problems?")
2. Process not Person — "Can you describe how we can improve the process?" (not "Who's at fault?")
3. Root Cause not Symptom — "What do you think is causing the delays?" (not "Why can't you do this?")

## GROW Model

- **Goal:** Define short/long term goals (Point B)
- **Reality:** Explore current situation (Point A, SWOT)
- **Options:** Identify & evaluate different action strategies (Brainstorm)
- **Way Forward:** How do we achieve it (Action Plan)

### Sample GROW Questions

**GOAL:** What results do you want to achieve? What is the perfect outcome? What do you really want? What will reaching the goal give you?

**REALITY:** What is happening now? What obstacles do you see? On a scale of 1-10, where are you? What is working well right now?

**OPTIONS:** What can you do to change the situation? What have you not tried? What would happen if you do nothing? What other approaches can bring success?

**WAY FORWARD:** Which option would be most successful? When precisely are you going to start? How will you monitor your progress? Will you do it?

## Components of a Good Goal — The 3 A's

1. **ALIGNED** — to Values & Declaration (Personal Declaration, Intent, Values, Emotional Peg)
2. **ABIDES** by SMART — Specific, Motivational, Attainable, Risk, Trackable
3. **AGREED** — by Coach & Coachee

### ALIGNED = Declaration → Values → Goal → Emotional Peg

**Typical Flow:**
1. **Declaration** — "What's your declaration?" (e.g., "I inspire greatness by living my greatness")
2. **Values** — "In achieving your goal, what values do you want to experience as an expression of your Declaration?" (e.g., Commitment, Responsibility, Joy)
3. **Goal** — "If you fully embrace your declaration and your values, what can you accomplish?" (GROW + 3A's)
4. **Emotional Peg** — "What does this goal mean to you?" (the reason behind the physical manifestation)

### Intent
An intention is something you **mean** to do — your goal, purpose, or aim. It doesn't require knowing HOW at the moment of intention.

### Emotional Peg
- Identifies the most basic thing the coachee wants to accomplish
- It's the reason behind the physical manifestation of the goal
- Clear and focused
- Obviously creates an emotional experience

## Radical Candor (Kim Scott)

- **Vertical axis:** "Care Personally" (Give a Damn)
- **Horizontal axis:** "Challenge Directly" (Willing to address hard truths)

| Quadrant | Description | Result |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| High Care + High Challenge | **Radical Candor** | Profound Change (GROWTH) |
| High Care + Low Challenge | Ruinous Empathy | No Change (IGNORANCE) |
| Low Care + High Challenge | Obnoxious Aggression | Might Change (DEFENSIVENESS) |
| Low Care + Low Challenge | Manipulative Insincerity | No Change (MISTRUST) |

## Giving & Receiving Feedback

**Giving — ABCDE:**
- **Ask** Permission
- **Behavior** Observed
- **Consequence** of Behavior
- **Determine** Options
- **Empower** Individual

**Receiving — THANKS:**
- **Thanks** for the Feedback
- **Hear** and clarify
- **Acknowledge** the sentiment or action

- **Keep Agreements** reached
- **Support** offered or needed

## Feedback vs Coaching

| Feedback | Coaching |
|----------|----------|
| Focused on past behavior | Focused on future behavior |
| Evaluative | Developmental |
| Telling or advice oriented | Inquiry oriented |
| Reinforces/changes specific behavior | Guides overall performance improvement |

## Socio-Cognitive Theory — How People Change

People choose how to behave. Change depends on awareness of 3 things:
1. **Value** — What is the value of changing?
2. **Prices They Pay** — What is the cost of NOT changing?
3. **Ability to Change** — Can they actually change?

## Drama Triangle → Empowerment Triangle

| Role | Behavior | Move To |
|------|----------|---------|
| Persecutor (Bully) | Critical, blames, controls | **Challenger** (Clear Structure) |
| Rescuer (Saviour) | "You need my help", fixes for others | **Coach** (Clear Support) |
| Victim (Helpless) | "Poor me", powerless, avoids decisions | **Survivor/Thriver** (Problem Solving) |

**Key coaching move:** "I care about you and I know you are capable. Do nothing that the other can do for him or herself."

## Group Leadership Functions (4 Dimensions)

1. **Emotional Stimulation (ES):** Revealing feelings, challenging behavior, inspiring others
2. **Caring (C):** Showing understanding, praising, accepting, giving emotionally
3. **Meaning Attribution (MA):** Clarifying feelings, interpreting, summarizing, helping understand experience
4. **Executive Function (EF):** Setting limits, managing time, eliciting reactions, dealing with decision-making

**Scores:** 41+ High | 16-40 Moderate | 15 Low
